EPUB Tsumego Template

The project reference example is here.

Ever since I was able to download GoBooks' EPUB books, I am in love with digital Go books. It's just so much more practical and speedier to read with them, specially due to the infinite scrolling that I wish all my books were EPUBs now.

But note that this is different from PDF. PDFs were made to be printed, it's the printable document format after all, they were actually not made to be read on a screen. EPUBs, on the other hand, offer basically everything you would want when using a digital screen. Sometimes, they might seem ugly, but that's usually due to the reader software, not the ebook itself. Elegant, aesthetically pleasing readers are not that trivial to find unfortunately, but here are 3 I find pretty and good enough:

  • Thorium
  • Lithium
  • (i)Books

Writing Go ebooks, unless they were PDFs — which you can reference in — eluded me for quite while, because I just had no idea how they were made. But, luckily, after accidentally mentioning my incompetence in this realm in an email to Anders Kierulf himself — founder of GoBooks and many other Go-related ventures — he mentioned they were simply HTML files, sometimes packed with CSS and JS as well, which makes total sense.

Then another lightbulb moment came to me and I realized they could be written in a very simple way using 2 other tools:

  • Pandoc
  • GoWrite2

With Pandoc, the Go writer is able to create the ebook in Markdown, which greatly reduces the clutter when writing; and, with GoWrite2, the diagrams can be created as vector images, which are not affected by pixelation. Also, with a little bit more editing, Pandoc is also able to create a PDF version of the ebook.

GoBooks EPUBs are still superior though, in my opinion. That's simply because their diagrams are much more interactive and suitably designed than the static vector diagrams GoWrite2 exports. However, I believe that isn't that big of an issue if we simply are able to get the job done with Pandoc + GoWrite2.

So, with that, we're all set! Check out this example as reference! I included some EPUB books there if you would like to study the topic more.
